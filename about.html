Yesterday I was out at a bar, and I overheard a conversation I’ve had increasingly often with myself over the last year. A bearded Brit behind me said to his friend, “My dream is to be on a beach somewhere, sipping a Pina Colada, typing away on my laptop for my freelance programming gig.” Just replace his Pina Colada with a French 75, and this is me.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment for software engineers is projected to grow a staggering 24% by 2026. Chat with any high-and-tight sporting bro wearing Birkestocks and you'll know about his office's beer fridges, work from home policy, and, most importantly, bring your dog to work day (spoiler alert: it's everyday). So I'm jumping on the developer bandwagon.

I started this blog to document my dive into coding. Over the next few months I'm going to attempt to:

learn how to code

learn how to decode coding culture

find my bliss on a beach somewhere, French 75 in hand, typing away on my laptop for my freelance programming gig.

So cheers to the dream and wish me luck. Let's see how it goes.
